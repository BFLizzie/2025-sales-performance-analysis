## Description
This project analyses 2025 sales transaction data to understand revenue outcomes, sales volume, and pricing behaviour across regions, products, customers, and sales representatives. The analysis focuses on how units sold and unit pricing combine to drive total revenue and overall sales performance.

## Objective
The objective of this analysis is to evaluate 2025 sales performance by examining revenue distribution, pricing differences, and sales volume across regions, products, and customers to support revenue optimisation and performance improvement.

## Business Questions
The analysis was guided by the following questions:
1. How do units sold vary across regions?
2. Are there regional differences in the unit price of product?
3. Which customers generate the highest total revenue?
4. Which products and categories contribute most to overall revenue?
5. How do units sold by product vary across regions?
6. What is the total, maximum, and minimum revenue values?
7. How does total revenue vary across regions?
8. Which sales representatives generate the highest and lowest revenue?

## Data Cleaning and Preparation
The dataset was prepared to ensure accuracy and consistency before analysis.
- Duplicate records were identified and removed
- Text fields such as Region, Product, Category, Sales Representative, and Customer were standardised
- Date values were verified and formatted correctly
- Numeric fields including Units Sold and Unit Price were validated
- A Total Revenue column was created using Units Sold multiplied by Unit Price
- Maximum Revenue and Minimum Revenue were calculated from Total Revenue.
- Missing and blank values were handled appropriately

## Dashboard
An Excel dashboard was developed to summarise key performance indicators, including:
- Revenue and units sold across regions
- Product and category contribution to revenue
- Customer revenue performance

## Key Insights
- Total revenue generated in 2025 was **₦302,680,445**, with the highest revenue **115,542,487** and lowest revenue **39,490,000** indicates significant variability in revenue distribution.
- The West region leads in both unit price **8,812,145** and total revenue **115,542,487**.
- The North region records higher average unit prices, suggesting a higher value product mix.
- Laptops **134,375,000** and Electronics **63,689,500** are the primary drivers of revenue.
- Revenue is concentrated among a small number of customers and sales representatives.

## Recommendation
- Conduct further analysis of the West region to understand the factors contributing to its higher revenue performance. 
- Review sales patterns among top revenue-generating customers to identify characteristics associated with higher-value sales. 
- Assess the performance of lower-revenue products and categories to identify growth opportunities. 
- Monitor revenue concentration among customers and sales representatives to improve revenue distribution. 
- Continue tracking regional pricing differences to better understand their relationship with revenue generation.

## Conclusion
Sales performance in 2025 is largely driven by a few key regions, products, and high value customers. The West region leads in both total revenue and the unit prices, indicating strong sales activity within that region. Products such as Laptops and Electronics contribute significantly to overall revenue, highlighting their importance to the company’s sales performance. However, the concentration of revenue among a limited number of customers and sales representatives suggests the need to expand the customer base to achieve more balanced and sustainable revenue growth.
